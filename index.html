Who We Are

We are a team of researchers from The New School's Graduate Program in International Affairs in New York City, committed to devoting our energies in service of refugees and asylum seekers in the United States and across the globe.

Throughout the Summer of 2015, we have been working with the International Rescue Committee as part of the United Nations Summer Program to develop innovative new ways of measuring the economic integration of refugees who flee dangerous environments and resettle with asylum in the United States.

What We Do:

We want to listen to refugees' experiences, perspectives and needs in order to support the IRC's work in helping those who have fled from harm to lead fulfilling, self-sufficient lives participating in their new homes and communities.

We are working with the IRC to develop a survey and interview process that will look at refugees' well-being and economic integration through a multi-dimensional lens.

Where We Work

International Rescue Committee’s Resettlement Communities

Why Are We Doing This?

We collect information on the economic factors and indicators that facilitate client economic integration in their resettled communities. We don’t know much about how social and economic factors shape economic possibilities for IRC resettled clients and would like to know more. The long term goal is to help clients become economically empowered and self-sufficient.
